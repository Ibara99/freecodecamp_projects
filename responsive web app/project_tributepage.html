A Tribute Page:
Bacharuddin Jusuf Habibie

“Without love, intelligence is dangerous. Without intelligence, love won't be enough.”--B. J. Habibie

Here is Habibie's Timeline:

1936
Habibie was born in Pare-pare, South Sulawesi, Indonesia.
1960
Habibie received an engineer's degree in Germany with the title Diplom-Ingenieur.
1962
Married his childhood friend, Hasri Ainun, and returning to Germany shortly afterwards.
1965
In 1965, Habibie delivered his dissertation in aerospace engineering and received the grade of "very good," giving him the title Doktoringenieur (Dr.-Ing.)
1974
in 1974, He was promoted to vice president of the Messerschmitt-Bölkow-Blohm, a West German aerospace manufacturer, in Hamburg.
1976
in 1976, Habibie was made CEO of the new state-owned enterprise Industri Pesawat Terbang Nurtanio (IPTN; Nurtanio Aircraft Industry), which later is known as Indonesian Aerospace (PT. Dirgantara Indonesia) since 2000.
1978
In 1978, he was appointed as State Minister of Research and Technology (Indonesian: Menteri Negara Riset dan Teknologi, Menristek)
1995
Under Habibie's leadership, IPTN became a manufacturer of aircraft including Puma helicopters and CASA planes. It pioneered a small passenger airplane, the N-250 Gatotkaca, in 1995
March 1998
He became Vice president in the Seventh Development Cabinet
May 1998
just two months into Habibie's vice presidency, Suharto announced his resignation. Habibie, as the Constitution says, succeeded him as president, until the next president election that was held in 1999.
2019
In early September 2019, he was admitted to Gatot Soebroto Army Hospital, where he was undergoing treatments for heart problems, namely cardiomyopathy, and died on 11 September 2019. He was buried the next afternoon at the Kalibata Heroes Cemetery, next to his wife's grave.
